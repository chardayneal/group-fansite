Gospel of Guac

A Dip Worthy of Worship

Beyond its popularity as a modern dip, guacamole holds deep cultural significance, symbolizing fertility, communal gathering, and centuries-old culinary tradition.

Guacamole originated with the Aztecs, who created a simple “avocado sauce” called āhuacamolli by mashing avocados with salt and sometimes tomatoes or chiles. Rich in nutrients and unique in flavor, guacamole became a treasured staple in Aztec cuisine

For the Aztecs, guacamole held deep symbolic meaning, as avocados represented fertility, love, and abundance due to their rich texture and growth in pairs.

Sharing guacamole in communal meals strengthened social bonds and celebrated the Aztecs' connection to the earth, making it a symbol of unity and abundance in their society.
